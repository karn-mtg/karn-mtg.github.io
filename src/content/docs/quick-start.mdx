# Quick Start

This guide walks you through creating your first deck from scratch. Before you begin, make sure you've completed [Installation](/getting-started) and the Arsenal setup.

## Create a deck

<Steps>
  1. Open Karn Forge. You'll land on the **Dashboard**.
  2. Click **New Deck** — either the `+` card in the deck grid or the button in the top bar.
  3. Give your deck a name, choose a format (e.g., Commander), and click **Create**.
</Steps>

## Add cards to your deck

<Steps>
  1. The **Deck Workshop** opens with an empty canvas on the left and a **Card Search** panel on the right.
  2. Type a card name in the search box. Results appear as you type, powered by the local Scryfall database.
  3. Click the **+** button on a search result to add it to your deck, or drag the result card directly onto the canvas.
  4. The card appears on the canvas and is added to your deck list.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
  Press `Ctrl+F` (Windows/Linux) or `Cmd+F` (macOS) at any time to focus the Card Search panel without using the mouse.
</Aside>

## Organize cards with groups

Groups are labeled containers on the canvas that help you visualize your deck's strategy.

<Steps>
  1. Press **G** or click the **Group** button in the canvas toolbar.
  2. Give the group a name (e.g., "Ramp") and pick a color.
  3. Drag cards from the canvas into the group, or enable **Pre-select by type** in the group creation dialog to automatically populate the group.
</Steps>

## Add a sticky note

<Steps>
  1. Press **N** or click the **Note** button in the canvas toolbar.
  2. A sticky note appears on the canvas. Click it and start typing.
  3. Sticky notes support Markdown: use `#` for headings, `**bold**`, `_italic_`, and `- lists`.
  4. Click anywhere outside the note to save it.
</Steps>

## Save your deck

Your deck auto-saves every 1.5 seconds after any change. You can also save manually with `Ctrl+S` / `Cmd+S`.

Canvas arrangements (the layout of cards, groups, notes, and widgets) are saved separately from the deck list. You can create multiple named arrangements for the same deck — for example, one for gameplay and one for a visual overview.
